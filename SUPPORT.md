# CleanCheck – Support Operations & SLA Manual (v1.0.0)

This manual outlines the service level agreements, release integrity policies, and operational support procedures for enterprise installations of **CleanCheck v1.0.0**.

---

## 1. Service Level Agreements (SLA)

CleanCheck Software Solutions, LLC guarantees the following response and resolution targets based on issue classification:

| Severity | Definition & Impact | Target Response | Target Resolution | Escalation Path |
| :--- | :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **P1 - Critical** | **System Unavailable**: The entire app is down, or inspectors are completely blocked from scanning QR codes or submitting inspections, with no workaround available. | **1 Hour** | **4 Hours** | Lead Security Engineer & Operations Director |
| **P2 - High** | **Core Feature Failure**: Essential workflows (e.g., supervisor verification, Google Sheets sync, or photo uploads) are failing, but workarounds exist (e.g., offline cache, manual sync button). | **4 Hours** | **1 Business Day** | Senior Systems Architect |
| **P3 - Medium** | **Non-Blocking Defect**: A feature works incorrectly but doesn't block daily cleaning operations (e.g., sorting on reporting lists is slow, or specific export formats show minor style misalignments). | **1 Business Day** | **3 Business Days** | Support Specialist |
| **P4 - Low** | **Cosmetic / Minor Enhancement**: Minor visual alignment requests, wording changes in guides, or suggestions for future feature integrations. | **3 Business Days** | **Next Scheduled Patch** | Product Management |

## 3. Customer Feedback Classification Policy

To maintain a predictable release cycle and prevent scope creep from destabilizing the stable **v1.0.x** codebase, customer feedback during pilot programs must be triaged strictly into three separate queues:

1.  **Defect / Bug (Blockers & Regression Fixes)**:
    -   *Criteria*: The software fails to act according to the specifications documented in `DOCUMENTATION.md` (e.g., GPS coordinates fail to render in the supervisor panel).
    -   *Action*: Patched immediately in `hotfix/` branch and released as **v1.0.x**.
2.  **Usability Friction (Enhancements & Polish)**:
    -   *Criteria*: The feature works as specified, but users experience confusion (e.g., "Add a larger, clearer error indicator when camera permissions are denied").
    -   *Action*: Collected during Week 3 of the pilot and queued for **v1.1.0** release planning.
3.  **New Feature Requests (Scope Additions)**:
    -   *Criteria*: Requests for features completely outside the v1.0 scope (e.g., "Add an NFC-tag scan fallback", "Integrate automated email triggers for P1 failed ratings").
    -   *Action*: Triaged to the **Product Advisory Board** for potential roadmap placement in **v1.2.x** or **v2.0.0**.

## 4. Disaster Recovery & Restorations Validation

To verify the integrity of automatic backups generated by `backup.sh`, administrators must execute a "Dry Run Restoration" on a separate staging environment every 30 days:

1.  Provision a clean staging container container using the `docker-compose.yml` blueprint.
2.  Import a recent backup archive generated by `backup.sh`:
    ```bash
    ./restore.sh backups/cleancheck_backup_latest.tar.gz
    ```
3.  Validate that the application successfully recovers the active tenant environment variable settings and client offline configurations.
4.  Log the validation event in the corporate compliance directory under `verification_logs.audit`.
